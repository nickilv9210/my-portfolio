Heat Maps

This page contains code I developed to generate a series of heat maps analyzing employment concentration. The code takes raw data downloaded from Lightcast, formats it to make data access and analysis more efficient, and then generates the heat maps. It is designed to be highly dynamic—rather than requiring a predefined list of states, it automatically scans a folder for files with state names and builds maps based on the available data. Two of the maps cover 10 states—Texas, Oklahoma, Arkansas, Missouri, Tennessee, Georgia, Florida, Kentucky, Ohio, and Indiana—highlighting where employment concentration is higher for two SOC job codes: Chemical Equipment Operators and Tenders and Production Workers, All Other. The other two maps focus specifically on the state of Tennessee, showing county-level heat maps of employment concentration for these same two job categories across each Tennessee county.
